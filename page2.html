Top 5 Place to Visit in Kuching, Sarawak

Updated january, 2019

Sarawak Cultural Village

Experience life in a traditional Sarawak village on this 4-hour tour from Kuching, including hotel pick-up. Learn about Sarawak’s diverse cultural heritage as you enjoy traditional ethnic dances and see demonstrations of handicrafts from bamboo carving to straw weaving. Watch the villagers go about their everyday lives, or get involved and learn how to shoot a Penan blowpipe or play an Orang Ulu sape.

Semonggoh Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre

Spare yourself lengthy jungle treks or painful public transport explorations and see orangutans in their natural habitat the easy way—on a guided door-to-door tour to the Semonggoh Nature Reserve. Learn how naturalists have helped these orange primates recover from captivity or injury, watch them enjoy their food—and maybe even catch sight of a mother and her baby. Choose between morning and afternoon tours.

Bako National Park

Explore the primary forest of Bako National Park on a day trip from Kuching to the Muara Tebas Peninsula, where you’ll find diverse plants, wildlife, and ecosystems. Travel by coach and boat to Bako National Park, watch for monkeys, lemurs, slow loris, and many more animals as you enter the forest, then enjoy a local-style lunch at the park’s canteen. With park entrance fees, transport, and lunch included, pickup and drop-off is available in Kuching and the Damai Peninsula, for a fascinating look at Sarawak’s wild side.

Sarawak Jong's Crocodile Farm

A few minutes' walk from the Tua Pek Kong Temple and Kuching Waterfront is the Main Bazaar, which is one of the oldest streets in Kuching. The Main Bazaar area has a row of two-storey shophouses that runs parallel to the main road and the waterfront. These old shophouses were built before World War II. Here, visitors can find a variety of native handicraft items including necklaces, bags, wood sculptures, rattan mats, baskets, ceramics, pottery items and native hunting weapons.
